The problem

Too many people lack reliable computers while usable devices sit in drawers.

Schoolwork, job applications, banking, telehealth, and basic communication all require technology. But many families cannot afford a dependable device.

Tech Rescue Corps turns unused technology into real opportunity through a safe, transparent refurbishment program.

What we do

A practical service model with measurable impact.

01

Collect

We accept donated laptops, desktops, monitors, keyboards, mice, and chargers from families, schools, and local businesses.

02

Secure

Every donated device goes through a documented data-wipe process before it is repaired, reinstalled, or donated.

03

Refurbish

Volunteers test components, install clean software, replace basic parts when possible, and prepare devices for everyday use.

04

Distribute

We partner with schools and community organizations to match refurbished devices with students and families who need them most.

How it works

Simple enough to launch. Serious enough to be trusted.

1

Device intake

Donors complete a quick intake form and confirm they understand that all storage drives will be wiped.

2

Inventory and tagging

Each device receives a tracking ID so volunteers can document status, repairs, and final placement.

3

Data security

Storage drives are wiped or removed before repair. Devices that cannot be safely reused are responsibly recycled.

4

Testing and setup

Volunteers check Wi-Fi, battery, keyboard, screen, ports, webcam, and performance before installation is finalized.

5

Community handoff

Devices are distributed through trusted school counselors, libraries, nonprofits, and community partners.

Donate technology

Your old device may be someone else's first real opportunity.

We are looking for working or repairable laptops and desktops. Devices should ideally be less than eight years old, but we will review older equipment case by case.

Most needed items

  • Laptops with chargers
  • Desktops with power cables
  • Monitors, keyboards, and mice
  • USB Wi-Fi adapters
  • SSD drives and RAM upgrades

Volunteer roles

You do not need to be a tech expert to help.

Repair Team

Test hardware, install software, replace basic parts, and prepare devices for donation.

Intake Team

Track donations, label devices, organize chargers, and maintain clean inventory records.

Outreach Team

Contact local schools, businesses, libraries, and community groups for partnerships.

Training Team

Create quick-start guides and help recipients learn basic computer safety and setup.

Trust matters

Our non-negotiables

Data safety: every drive is wiped or removed before reuse.

Transparency: every device is tracked from donation to placement.

Equity: devices go to people with demonstrated need through partner referrals.

Responsibility: unusable electronics are recycled through approved channels.
